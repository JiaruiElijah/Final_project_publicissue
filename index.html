Intro

That ethics most generally speaking has a say in matters of technology, or that technology is subject to ethical considerations, follows from the simple fact that technology is an exercise of human power, that is, a form of action, and all human action is answerable to moral scrutiny. It is also a truism that one and the same power can be for good and for evil, and that in wielding it one can abide by ethical norms or violate them. Technology, as vastly enhanced human power,clearly falls under this general truth. But does it constitute a special case, requiring an effort of ethical thought differentfrom that which befits every human action and has been sufficient for all its kinds in the past? My thesis is that it does indeed constitute a novel and special case...
---"Technology as a Subject for Ethics", by Hans Jonas
